Inequalities in funding

According to the Institute for Fiscal Studies, available funding for further education is heavily restricted and compares badly with the higher education sector.1 School sixth forms have had to deal with budget cuts of 23% per student since 2010-11, and further education and sixth from college funding per student has fallen by about 12% over the same time period. Despite a further Â£300 million being allocated for 2020-21, spending per student in further education is over 7% down on 2010. Further education colleges and sixth forms have been subject to the largest cuts in recent years.
